Welcome to Bimbarama Temple

Elukatiya, Ahangama - A sacred sanctuary for spiritual peace, meditation, and Buddhist heritage. Whether you seek tranquility, spiritual guidance, or a deeper connection to Buddhist traditions, our temple warmly welcomes visitors and devotees alike to experience its serene beauty and rich history.

About the Temple

Bimbarama Maha Viharaya, nestled in the lush surroundings of Elukatiya, Ahangama, is a historic Buddhist temple dating back several centuries. It stands as a beacon of peace and spiritual reflection for locals and travelers.

The temple grounds feature traditional architecture, ancient statues, and beautifully maintained meditation halls, inviting visitors to immerse themselves in a calm and reverent atmosphere.

Our community actively participates in preserving cultural heritage while promoting mindfulness, compassion, and wisdom.

History & Heritage

The temple's origins trace back over 400 years, serving as a spiritual hub through colonial times to modern Sri Lanka. It has witnessed numerous restorations and remains an important site preserving traditional Buddhist culture.

Stories and legends about the temple's founding monks and benefactors are passed down, contributing to the sacred aura of the site.

Architecture Highlights

The temple complex showcases classic Sri Lankan Buddhist architecture, featuring:

  • Stupas with intricate stone carvings
  • Wooden pillars and ornate ceiling murals
  • A meditation hall designed for peaceful contemplation
  • Ancient Buddha statues with unique local artistic styles

Visitors appreciate the harmony between nature and temple design, reflecting spiritual principles of balance and serenity.

Daily Rituals

At Bimbarama Temple, daily rituals are designed to foster spiritual growth and communal harmony. Visitors are encouraged to participate and observe the rhythms of temple life:

  • Morning Puja (5:30 AM - 7:00 AM): Begin the day with chanting, meditation, and offerings to the Buddha.
  • Midday Blessings (12:00 PM): Monks perform blessings and teachings, open to all who seek guidance.
  • Evening Offering (6:00 PM - 7:30 PM): Devotees offer alms and join in the chanting to conclude the day with gratitude.
  • Dharma Discussions: Scheduled on weekends, these sessions deepen understanding of Buddhist teachings.

All are welcome to join respectfully and experience the spiritual vibrancy of the temple community.

Upcoming Events & Festivals

Join us for vibrant celebrations that highlight Buddhist traditions and community spirit, including:

  • Vesak Festival: Illuminating the temple with lanterns and commemorating the Buddha's birth, enlightenment, and passing.
  • Poson Poya: Marking the introduction of Buddhism to Sri Lanka with religious observances and processions.
  • Annual Kathina Ceremony: Offering robes and gifts to the monastic community, fostering generosity and merit.

Special events are announced on our social media and notice boards.

Visitor Information

We warmly welcome visitors of all backgrounds. Here are some helpful tips for your visit:

  • Dress modestly: shoulders and knees covered.
  • Remove footwear before entering temple buildings.
  • Photography is allowed outside the main shrines; please ask before taking photos of monks or ceremonies.
  • Respect silence during meditation and rituals.
  • Guided tours are available by prior arrangement.

Contact us to arrange group visits or participate in volunteer programs.
